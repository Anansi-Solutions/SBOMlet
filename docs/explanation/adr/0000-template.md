# ADR-0000: Short title of the decision

- **Status:** Proposed | Accepted | Superseded by [ADR-NNNN](NNNN-....md)
- **Date:** YYYY-MM-DD

## Context and problem

What forced a decision here? Describe the situation and the tension in it —
the constraints, the thing that didn't work, or the question we had to answer —
in a few plain sentences. Keep it at the level of *what* and *why*, not *how*.

## Decision drivers

The handful of things that actually mattered for the choice. For example:

- a constraint the tool must hold to (determinism, minimal dependencies, …)
- a correctness property we cannot give up
- effort or maintenance cost

## Considered options

1. **Option A** — one line.
2. **Option B** — one line.
3. **Option C** — one line.

## Decision

The option we chose, and the reason in a sentence or two. Lead with the reason a
reader cares about, not the mechanism.

If a brief comparison helps, add it here — a line per option on the one driver
that decided it. Don't write a full pros-and-cons matrix unless the decision was
genuinely close.

## Consequences

- **Good:** what this buys us.
- **Bad / cost:** what it costs, and what we accept by choosing it.
- **Neutral:** follow-on facts a future reader should know.
